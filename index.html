🏨 Lulu Hotel · Anaheim CA

You've never used Lightning Lane. Disney replaced the old free FastPass with a paid system in 2021. Two separate products — understanding them before Sunday saves hours and real money.

🎫
Lightning Lane Multi Pass (LLMP)
~$30/person · The main one.

Buy in Disney app the moment the park opens (7 AM). Pick one ride at a time. After tapping in OR after 2 hours — whichever comes first — book the next. Cycles all day.

Best for: Indiana Jones, Space Mountain, Matterhorn, Haunted Mansion, Smuggler's Run.
⚡
Lightning Lane Single Pass (LLSP)
$20-25/person per ride.

For premium rides only: Rise of the Resistance, Radiator Springs Racers. Very limited slots. Buy one at entry, another after using it.

Your move: Skip this. Do Rise at rope drop in standby — shorter wait, no extra charge.
⏱️ SUNDAY STRATEGY — STEP BY STEP
1
Saturday Night — Set Up the App
Disneyland app open. Log in. Add park ticket. Pre-load payment. Set alarm for 6:45 AM. This prevents Sunday morning chaos.
2
7:00 AM Sunday — Buy LLMP at Park Entry
The moment you tap in, buy Multi Pass (~$30). Immediately book Indiana Jones Adventure, 9:30-10:30 AM window.
3
8:00 AM — Rope Drop → Galaxy's Edge
Don't waste LLMP on Rise of the Resistance. Rope drop standby = 20-40 min. By 10 AM it's 60-120 min. Walk fast, queue immediately. Best ride, no extra charge.
4
KEY UNLOCK: Tap In → Book Next Immediately
The moment you physically tap in to a Lightning Lane ride, open the app and book your next one. Don't wait 2 hours. Tap in → book next → cycle. Indiana Jones → Matterhorn → Space Mountain → Haunted Mansion → repeat.
5
Check App Wait Times Constantly
The app shows real-time standby waits. Under 20 min: walk up. Over 45 min: use your LL booking. This math maximizes your day automatically.
6
Power Move: Modify Time Windows Freely
You can change a LL booking's time window without losing your next-booking ability. Not near Indiana Jones at 10 AM? Move it to noon. Most first-timers never know this exists.

YOUR FRAME OF REFERENCE
You've been to Cedar Point (world's coaster capital — intensity, records, minimal theming) and Kings Island (The Beast, Diamondback, solid mix). Knott's is closest to Kings Island in vibe — good coasters, real theming, excellent food. But it has something neither park has: Ghost Town, an actual 1880s Old West village. Knott's is also America's oldest theme park — it predates Disneyland.
🎢 RIDES — IN ORDER OF PRIORITY
HangTime ⚡ — DO THIS FIRST
★★★★★
Inverted coaster with a holding brake at the top of a 96° drop that suspends you for 2-3 seconds — dangling, looking straight down — before releasing. The anticipation is legitimately terrifying. Under 90 seconds total but that pause stays with you.
🔄 Nothing like this at Cedar Point or Kings Island — unique to Knott's
GhostRider 🪵 — MAIN EVENT
★★★★★
4,533 ft wooden coaster, 108 ft tall, 56 mph. Wild, loud, classically rough. If you loved The Beast at Kings Island, this is its California cousin. Renovated in 2016 — much smoother now. Front row for views, back row for the wild ride.
🔄 Direct comparison to The Beast (Kings Island) — same big wooden coaster energy
Silver Bullet 🌀
★★★★
Suspended looping coaster, feet dangle through 6 inversions. Smooth, fast, great flying sensation. Less intense than Raptor at Cedar Point but genuinely excellent.
🔄 Like a smoother, compact Raptor (CP) or Banshee (Kings Island)
Montezooma's Revenge 🔁
★★★
Classic 1978 shuttle coaster. Launches forward through a giant loop and spiral, then reverses backward. Short, punchy, 46 years on the menu.
🔄 Boomerang-style — larger version of the county fair staple
🍗 THE FOOD — THIS IS WHY THE PARK EXISTS
🍗
Mrs. Knott's Fried Chicken Dinner
Cordelia Knott started serving fried chicken in 1934. Crowds grew so large that Walter built amusements to entertain people waiting. That's how a theme park was born from a fried chicken dinner. Chicken, mashed potatoes, biscuits, boysenberry preserves. On the menu 90+ years. Order it.
🫐
Boysenberry Everything
Walter Knott developed the boysenberry here in the 1920s — blackberry/raspberry/loganberry hybrid. It's in everything: punch, pie, BBQ sauce, lemonade, jam. Get the boysenberry punch. The jam in the gift shop is a genuinely great bring-home gift.
